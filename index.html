About BRUR

Our History

Begum Rokeya University, Rangpur (BRUR) is a public university in Bangladesh, established in 2008. The university is named after Begum Rokeya, a prominent Bengali feminist thinker, writer, educator and political activist from British India.


The university started its academic activities with four departments under three faculties. At present, the university has 22 departments under six faculties. The university offers undergraduate and postgraduate programs leading to bachelor's, master's, and PhD degrees.


The campus is located at Rangpur, a divisional city in northern Bangladesh. The university aims to provide quality education and promote research in various fields of knowledge.


Our Vision

To be a center of excellence in higher education, research, and innovation that contributes to the development of the nation and the global community.


Our Mission

  • To provide quality education and foster research and innovation
  • To promote critical thinking and develop socially responsible citizens
  • To contribute to sustainable development through knowledge creation
  • To preserve and promote cultural heritage and values
